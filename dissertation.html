Project Overview

This study explored methods to enhance the engagement and comprehension of digital Terms and Conditions (T&C) among elderly individuals in India. It investigated the reasons behind the gap between older adults and digital technologies, focusing on privacy concerns as a key barrier.

Solution

The study proposes a multi-pronged approach:

  • Revamping T&C Design: This involves making the terms themselves easier to understand. We can achieve this by simplifying the language, using clear formatting, and incorporating visuals like icons or flowcharts. Additionally, summaries of key points and the ability to customize settings can further empower users.
  • Building Collaboration: To truly bridge the digital divide, a combined effort is needed. Legal teams and designers should work together to create user-friendly T&Cs. Educating users about commonly used legal terms can also be beneficial. Finally, policymakers have a role to play in ensuring transparency and protecting user rights, further empowering elderly users online.

Problem

Bridging the gap between elderly users and safe online experiences requires tackling the complexity and accessibility of Terms & Conditions (T&Cs). These terms are crucial for informed decision making, yet current challenges pose significant hurdles:

  • Dense Legal Jargon: Current T&Cs are often filled with complex legal language, making them difficult for anyone, especially older adults, to understand.
  • Accessibility for Impaired Vision: Many elderly users experience vision problems. The current format of T&Cs, often lengthy and dense with text, can make them difficult to read.
  • Lack of Engagement: The sheer length and dry presentation of T&Cs can be disengaging, leading users to skip them entirely, potentially putting themselves at risk.

Research Process

Literature Review

To understand the reasons behind the gap between older adults and digital technologies, researchers conducted a literature analysis. This analysis revealed three key concerns that can act as barriers:

  • The Digital Divide: This refers to the lack of access, skills, or knowledge needed to effectively use digital tools. Older adults may face challenges due to limited internet connectivity, affordability of devices, or a lack of training opportunities.
  • Psychological Hurdles: Beyond technical limitations, psychological factors can also play a role. Fear of failure, a perceived lack of technical ability, or a sense of being overwhelmed by the constant technological advancements can all contribute to discouragement.
  • Privacy Concerns: Data security and privacy issues are a growing concern for everyone, but can be especially worrisome for older adults who may be less familiar with online threats. Understanding how their data is collected and used can be a significant hurdle to adopting new technologies.

Design Process

A survey found older adults have significant privacy fears regarding digital technologies, including identity theft, data breaches, and financial loss. However, they often neglect to read T&Cs that could explain these risks. Building on this and prior research, the study designed three prototypes for payment gateway apps (like Gpay, a commonly used app among participants) using Figma, an online prototyping tool. Before testing, a survey gauged participants' familiarity with Gpay. Finally, researchers presented the prototypes to participants via WhatsApp links and Google Meet video calls.

User Testing

Following the pilot test, the research shifted to more comprehensive user testing phase:

  • Sample Size: The team recruited a larger group of fifteen participants. This broader sample size allows for a wider range of user experiences and perspectives to be captured.
  • Individual Sessions: Unlike the pilot test, user testing was conducted individually via Google Meet. This allows researchers to tailor the testing experience to each participant's needs and preferences, leading to richer data.
  • Data Collection:To gain deeper insights into user behavior and thought processes, the researchers recorded audio during each testing session. Analyzing these recordings can reveal user hesitations, thought patterns, and emotional responses to the prototypes, providing valuable feedback beyond what simple observations can capture.

Pilot Testing

The pilot testing stage aimed to identify and address any initial usability issues with the prototypes before presenting them to a wider audience. Here's a closer look:

  • Sample Size:A small group of two participants was chosen for the pilot test. This allows for focused observation and detailed feedback on each user's interaction with the prototypes.
  • Focus:The primary goal was to assess usability. Researchers observed how participants navigated the prototypes, identified any confusing elements, and ensured smooth functionality.
  • Outcome:The pilot test revealed a single issue: the background color of Prototype 2 was found to be distracting and negatively impacted usability. Based on this feedback, the researchers promptly addressed the problem by changing the background color from Yellow (#FDBD00) to a more user-friendly peach (#F3CEB1).

The quantitative analysis is done by measuring the time taken by each participant while testing and also by asking them to rate each prototype out of 5 based on readability and engagement using Likert scale rating method and calculated using the Spss software.

Results

Quantitative analysis

Key Findings:

According to quantitative analysis it has been found out that according to Readability the Prototype 3 is considered to be the significant one and according to engagement both second and third are considered to be the significant one.

  • Privacy Concerns and T&C Disconnect:Older adults express significant privacy anxieties regarding data breaches, identity theft, and financial loss when using digital technologies. However, they often neglect to read Terms & Conditions (T&C) documents that could address these very concerns.
  • Need for Multidisciplinary Solutions: Bridging the privacy gap for older adults requires a collaborative effort from various fields. Designers can create innovative T&C presentations, legal experts can simplify legal jargon, educators can explain legal terminology, and policymakers can ensure transparency and user rights protection.
  • Importance of User-Friendly T&Cs:Complex legal language and unengaging presentation formats in traditional T&Cs discourage many older adults from reading them. Innovative design approaches that utilize interactive elements, clear visuals, and concise summaries are crucial for improving T&C engagement.
  • Educating Older Users: Educational programs and resources specifically aimed at older adults can empower them to understand commonly used legal terms found in T&Cs. This knowledge can equip them to make informed decisions regarding their online privacy.
  • Policymakers' Role in Transparency:Policymakers have a responsibility to advocate for clearer and more user-friendly T&Cs. Additionally, establishing strong regulations to protect user data privacy is essential for creating a secure digital environment for older adults.

CONCLUSION

While investigating the gap between older adults and the digital devices and technologies , the study identified significant concerns about T&C complexity and lack of understanding among older adults, raising the question: "Why aren't people reading T&C pages?". The study found that lengthy, jargon-filled T&C pages contribute to older adults' avoidance of digital technologies. Simplifying the language, incorporating visuals, and maintaining a clear format can improve comprehension and engagement. However, the study acknowledges the limitations of a small participant pool and the need for further research.

While not directly addressing the initial question of bridging the entire digital technology gap, this study empowers older adults to make informed decisions through improved T&C accessibility. They can now consciously choose whether to use a service based on a clear understanding of its terms, reducing reliance on avoidance due to fear and confusion.
