Project Overview

"Globast," an inclusive movie streaming application was developed focused on providing an equivalent and enjoyable movie-watching experience for individuals with diverse abilities. The project began with an exploration of the challenges faced by users with disabilities on existing platforms, leading to a thorough disability analysis drawing from key literature papers.

Problem

  • Visual Impairment: Individuals experiencing difficulty seeing text, images, and navigating interfaces may find it challenging to interact with computers and digital devices in the usual way. This can include problems reading on-screen content due to blurry vision, difficulty distinguishing colors or contrast, or trouble perceiving the layout and functionality of buttons and menus. As a result, they might require assistance using traditional interfaces, such as someone magnifying text on a screen, reading content aloud, or providing guidance on navigating a program's features.

  • Auditory Impairment: Trying to decipher what's happening on screen can be frustrating when the audio isn't cooperating. The dialogue might be mumbled and faint, lost in a sea of booming sound effects or an overpowering soundtrack. This uneven mix makes it difficult to follow the plot, catch jokes, or appreciate the emotional moments. Whether it's a movie, TV show, or even a video game, this struggle to hear all the audio elements can leave you feeling disconnected from the experience.

  • Physical Impairment: Physical limitations due to conditions like arthritis, tremors, or carpal tunnel syndrome can make precise movements or gripping objects difficult. Similarly, reduced dexterity or weakness in the hands and arms can impede using a keyboard or manipulating a mouse. Touchscreens can also be challenging for users with limited hand-eye coordination or tremors, as selecting small targets accurately can be frustrating. Even for people without physical limitations, some users may find touchscreens less intuitive than physical keys, especially for extended typing or complex tasks.

  • Cognitive Impairment: Sometimes we can hit a wall when trying to take in too much information at once. This can be especially true with complex instructions. When faced with a lot of details or steps, it might feel like our brains are overloaded. This can lead to confusion, difficulty understanding the overall task, and even forgetting important parts of the instructions. It's like trying to fill a cup with water from a fire hose - the water just spills over the brim!

Solution

  • Visual Impairment: For users with visual impairments, navigating the Globast interface can be difficult. To ensure readability, Globast utilizes high contrast design with the ability to resize text. Integration with screen readers allows content to be vocalized, while image descriptions convey visual information. Additionally, Globast explores offering Braille or tactile interfaces for users who cannot see the screen.

  • Auditory Impairment: For users with auditory impairments, following dialogue and sound effects can be challenging. Globast provides closed captions and subtitles, with customization options for size, color, and font. Descriptive narration, known as audio description, explains non-verbal auditory cues like sound effects and background noises. Sign language interpretation videos are also offered for users who rely on sign language. Advanced volume controls allow users with hearing loss to amplify sound.

  • Physical Impairment: Globast recognizes that using a mouse, keyboard, or touchscreen can be difficult for users with physical impairments. Globast offers voice control, allowing users to navigate and control playback with voice commands. Keyboard shortcuts provide alternative navigation methods. For users with limited mobility, switch control enables operating the interface with specialized switches. Globast is also exploring eye tracking technology for users who can control the interface through eye movements. UI elements are designed with larger size and spacing for easier interaction.

  • Cognitive Impairment: Understanding complex instructions and processing information overload can be difficult for users with cognitive impairments. Globast maintains a clean and uncluttered interface with clear and concise labels. A consistent layout across the platform reduces confusion, while clear and straightforward navigation options make browsing effortless. Content is organized logically and by category for easier exploration. Globast avoids overwhelming users with too many choices at once, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable streaming experience.

Research Process

Personas & Scenarios

Personas & Scenarios were developed to understand the diverse needs of potential users. These personas included Victor, a screenwriter with quadriplegia; Charles, a user with tunnel vision impairment; Yue, an entrepreneur with language preferences; and Mush, a dyslexic film journalist. These personas helped shape the design process

Methodology

The low-fidelity phase involved creating a paper prototype for the application. This prototype outlined the basic structure, user interface, and key features. The initial design focused on inclusivity, incorporating features like audio descriptions, subtitles, voice assistance, and blocked captions. This phase allowed for quick iterations and adjustments based on feedback.


Hi-Fidelity

The high-fidelity prototype evolved from the low-fidelity version, incorporating detailed design elements, color schemes, and user interface refinements. The design adhered to inclusive principles, ensuring that each feature was carefully implemented to cater to users with visual, auditory, physical, and cognitive impairments.

User Testing

User testing was conducted with 4 participants (visually and auditory impaired) to understand how well they interact with Globast, an inclusive movie streaming platform.


The testers were given 5 tasks to complete:

  • Find and play "The Batman" movie in the superhero genre.

  • Find and play "The Batman" movie with Chinese subtitles.

  • See the cast and crew for "The Batman" movie.

  • Change the audio description voice for "The Batman" to sound like Robert Pattinson.

  • Switch user profiles from Sherlock to Heisenberg.

The time to complete each task was recorded, and testers were asked for their feedback on the platform.After the main testing, there was an engagement usability test where participants could explore the platform freely and provide feedback on how engaging it was. They were given a questionnaire to fill out at the end.


Iterations

1. The scroll-to-top button was removed because it hid content sometimes. A sticky header was added instead, which stays visible as the user scrolls.

2. The way to select subtitle language and audio description voice was changed. Originally, users had to scroll through a list to find their preference. Now, they can type their preference into a search bar for faster selection.
